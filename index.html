Really Awesome Animals

Rabbit

The rabbit is a small, grazing herbivore. They are closely related to hares and pikas, and they have various species of rabbits found in 70 different families. Fun Fact: Rabbits sleep, on average, 11.4 hours a day, often with one eye open.

Koala

The koala is a marsupial native to the continent of Australia. They are known for feeding on eucalyptus leaves and their sleepy nature. The koala's closest living relative is the wombat.

Fun Fact: their name comes from the Dharug (an aboriginal ethnic group) word for no water.

Chameleon

The chameleon is a small reptile known for its camouflage ability. They typically live in trees, and they are insectivores. The camouflage of a chameleon can be a social, defensive, or environmental reaction. The chameleon is native to the continent of Africa, parts of the Middle East, and the southern tip of the Indian subcontinent.

Fun Fact: Chameleons are able to change their skin through subcutaneous pigments. The molecular mechanism that allows chameleons to tune the wavelength given off by these pigments is still unknown to science.

Octopus

The octopus is a an eight-armed marine animal. It is classified as a mollusc, sharing the phylum with snails and squids. The octopus is a predator with the ability to excrete ink, camouflage, and move quickly through water. All octopuses are venomous.

Snake

Fun Fact: Now sadly extinct, the longest discovered species of snake was the Titanoboa cerrejonensis, which was measured to be 42 ft long. The smallest snake measures to be a mere 4.1 in long, the leptotyphlops carlae.
